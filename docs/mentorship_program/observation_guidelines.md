# Classroom observation

1. Presented topics in a logical, well paced sequence? Do you believe a different sequence could benefit learning or give a different perspective?

2. Related lesson to previous material and used tecniques to assess previous knowledge?

3. Do you have similar examples that come from your personal experience? Or do you relate to examples give?

4. Do you believe visuals/handouts were effective ? Can they improve? how?

5. Do you see the theory of this course beeing applyed by the trainers? 
Give one or two examples where you observed it.

6. What parts of the material or approach of the session could be improved? 

7. quality of questions, discussion techniques, students participation. Can any questions or activities be improved? What can be done differently in a _in person_ set up ?

8. List 3 things that went very well in this session.

9. List things that can be improved.

10. Are there points that were not clear for you, or where clarity of the content could be improved?

11. Free reflexions are also encouraged.

25 Guided Observation Questions for Mentees
How clearly did the trainer communicate the learning outcomes, and how did these outcomes shape the flow of the session?

In what ways did the trainer activate or assess participants’ prior knowledge before introducing new content?

How logically and coherently was the session structured, and what impact did the pacing have on learning?

How clear and accessible were the trainer’s explanations of key concepts throughout the session?

Which examples or analogies used by the trainer were most effective, and why did they support understanding?

How well did the chosen teaching methods (lecture, discussion, group work, activities) align with the content being taught?

How would you describe the trainer’s facilitation style, and how did it influence the learning atmosphere?

How effective were the trainer’s questioning techniques in stimulating thinking, participation, or deeper understanding?

What strategies did the trainer use to maintain learner engagement, and how successful were they?

How did the trainer foster motivation during the session, and what seemed to resonate most with participants?

How were group dynamics managed, and what did the trainer do to support collaboration or address challenges?

How effective were the visuals (slides, diagrams, handouts) in supporting comprehension, and how could they be improved?

How well did the materials and resources used in the session contribute to learning?

Where did you observe the trainer applying the theories or principles being taught in the course itself? Provide one or two examples.

How did the trainer provide feedback during the session, and how did this feedback support learner progress?

What formative assessment techniques did the trainer use to check understanding, and how effective were they?

In what ways did the trainer create a learner‑centred environment, and where could learner autonomy be increased?

How effectively did the trainer manage time, transitions, and the overall rhythm of the session?

What contributed to the emotional climate of the room, and how did the trainer influence feelings of safety, trust, or openness?

How inclusive was the session in terms of participation, accessibility, and representation of diverse perspectives?

How well did the trainer adapt to unexpected situations, learner needs, or emerging questions during the session?

How effectively did the trainer manage cognitive load (e.g., chunking content, scaffolding, pacing)?

What opportunities for practice or application were provided, and how well did they support skill development?

Which three aspects of the session were particularly strong, and what made them effective?

Which aspects of the session could be improved, and what concrete suggestions would you propose for enhancing them?
